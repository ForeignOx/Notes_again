## Lemma
Given a [[curves|path]] $\gamma:[a,b]\to U$ is a path in an [[open sets|open set]] $U\subseteq \mathbb{C}$, then there exists $r>0$, such that $\forall z\in\gamma$ we have $B_{r}(z)\subset U$
Furthermore, there exist $a=t_{1}<t_{2}<\dots<t_{N+1}=b$ such that
$$
\gamma \subset \bigcup_{n=1}^{N} B_{r}(\gamma(t_{n}))
$$
For $1\leq n\leq N$
Which is known as having a finite subcovering, and
$$
\gamma \big{|}_{[t_{n},t_{n+1}]} \subset B_{r}(\gamma(t_{n}))
$$
