## Definition
For $0\leq r<R\leq \infty$, and $a\in \mathbb{C}$, the set
$$
A_{r,R}(a)=\left\{ z\in \mathbb{C}:\middle|: r<\left| z-a \right| <R \right\}
$$
Is called the annulus with centre $a$, internal radius $r$, and external radius $R$
## Example
Because of the $\leq \infty$, these include, $\mathbb{C}^{*}=A_{0,\infty}(0)$, the punctured ball $A_{0,R}(a)=B_{R}(a)\setminus \left\{ a \right\}$
