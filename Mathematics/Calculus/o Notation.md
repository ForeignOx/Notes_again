## o Polynomial
Let $n$ be a positive integer. We say that a [[Functions|function]] 
$$
f(x)=o(x^{n})
$$
as $x\to0$ if 
$$
\lim_{ x \to 0 } \frac{f(x)}{x^{n}}=0
$$
In particular, for any constant $\alpha \neq 0$, $\alpha x^{m}=o(x^{n})$ iff $m>n$
