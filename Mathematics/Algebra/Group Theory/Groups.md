A group is a set, $G$, and a [[Binary Operations|binary operation]], $*:G\times G\to G$ such that the group, $(G,*)$, fulfils the following axioms:
- Closure - $\forall a,b\in G, a*b \in G$ (not strictly necessary given well defined binary operation)
- Associativity - $\forall a,b,c \in G, (a*b)*c=a*(b*c)$
- Identity - $\exists e\in G:  g*e=e*g=g, \forall g \in G$
- (Left) Inverse - $\forall g \in G, \exists g ^{-1}\in G:g^{-1}*g=e$
A finite group $~G$ is a group having finitely many elements, the order $\left| G \right|$ is the number of elements
___
Let $G$ be a group. $g,h\in G$, then $(g*h)^{-1}=h^{-1}g^{-1}$
## Examples
Finite [[Cyclic Groups|cyclic group]] $\mathbb{Z} /n$ under addition, $e=\overline{0}$
___
The infinite cyclic group, $\mathbb{Z}$ under addition, $(\mathbb{Z},+)$
___
Any field $\mathbb{F}$ under addition, $(\mathbb{F},+)$
___
The [[Vectorspace Rn|$(\mathbb{R}^{n},+)$]] or in fact any other [[Vectorspaces|vectorspace]] under addition; $(V,+)$
___
$(\mathbb{Z} /n)^{\times}$ is the set of units of $\mathbb{Z} /n$, so $((\mathbb{Z} /n)^{\times},\cdot)$ is a group, this works for any [[Rings|ring]]; $(R^{\times},\cdot)$
For example $\mathbb{R}^{\times}=\mathbb{R}\setminus \left\{ 0 \right\}$ forms a ring and this also works with any field; $\mathbb{F}^{\times}=\mathbb{F}\setminus \left\{ 0 \right\}$ forms a field under multiplication
Another example is $GL_{n}(\mathbb{F})=(M_{n}(\mathbb{F}))^{\times}$ is a group
