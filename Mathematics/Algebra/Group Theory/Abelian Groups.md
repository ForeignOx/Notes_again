## Definition
A binary operator of a [[Groups|group]] is commutative if:
$$
\forall a,b \in G, a*b=b*a
$$
If the binary operator of a group is commutative, then the group is said to be abelian

## Finitely Generated Abelian Groups
### Definition
$G$ is finitely generated if there exists a finite generator set of $G$
### Example
Any finite group is finitely generated. 
$\mathbb{Z}^{n}$ is finitely generated, which leads to if $G$ is finitely generated, then $G^{k}$ is finitely generated
$SL_{2}(\mathbb{Z})$ is generated by
$$
\begin{pmatrix}
1 & 1 \\
0 & 1 
\end{pmatrix},\begin{pmatrix}
0 & 1 \\
-1 & 0
\end{pmatrix}
$$
$\mathbb{Q}$ is not finitely generated
### Proposition
If $G$ is an abelian finitely generated group, then
$$
G=\left< s_{1},\dots,s_{n}\mid s_{i}s_{j}=s_{j}s_{i} \right> 
$$
Define $\varphi:\mathbb{Z}^{n}\to G$, $\varphi(a_{1},\dots,a_{n})=s_{1}^{a_{1}},s_{2}^{a_{2}}\dots s_{n}^{a_{n}}$
By the [[First Isomorphism Theorem|first isomorphism theorem]], $G\cong \mathbb{Z}^{n} /K$, where $K=\text{ker}(\varphi)$
### Theorem
Every finitely generated abelian group is isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}^{n }/k$, we call $K$ the relation group, $K\subseteq \mathbb{Z}^{n}$. If $K=\left\{ e \right\}$, $G$ is a free abelian group
### Example
$$
G=\left< a,b,c\mid \text{abelian,}a^{4}=e,b^{6}=e \right> 
$$
With $a\in \mathbb{Z} /4,b\in \mathbb{Z} /6,c \in \mathbb{Z}$, then
$$
G\cong \mathbb{Z} /4\times \mathbb{Z} /6\times \mathbb{Z}\cong \mathbb{Z} /2\times \mathbb{Z} /12\times \mathbb{Z}
$$
