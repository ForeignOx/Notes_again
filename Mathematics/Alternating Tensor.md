In $\mathbb{R}^{3}$, the alternating tensor, or Levi-Civita symbol is defined as
$$
\epsilon_{ijk}=\begin{cases}
0 & \text{if any of }i,j,k\text{ are equal} \\
1 & \text{if }(ijk)=(123),(231),(312) & \text{even/cyclic permutations of (123)} \\
-1 & \text{if }(ijk)=(132),(213),(321) & \text{odd permutations of (123)}
\end{cases}
$$
In $\mathbb{R}^{3}$, this has $\hspace{0pt}27$ components, but only $\hspace{0pt}3$ are non-zero
This can be extended to $\mathbb{R}^{n}$ by considering odd and even permutations of $(123,\dots,n)$
