The [[Sets|set]] difference between $A$ and $B$, sometimes called $A$ minus $B$, is the set consisting of the elements of $A$ which are not also in $B$. We write:
$$
A\setminus B=\{ x \in A|x \not\in B \}
$$
For example, $\mathbb{Z}\setminus \mathbb{N}=\{ 0,-1,-2,\dots \}$
