## Definition
Let [[Sets|$S$]] be a fixed [[Domain|domain]], and let $\mathfrak{F}$ be a family of [[Subsets|subsets]] of $S$
The intersection of $\mathfrak{F}$, or the intersection of all the sets in $\mathfrak{F}$ is the set of elements belonging to every set in $\mathfrak{F}$
We designate the intersection $\bigcap \mathfrak{F}$ or $\bigcap_{A\in \mathfrak{F}}A$, and thu we have:
$$
\bigcap \mathfrak{F}=\left\{ x\mid(\forall A^{\in \mathfrak{F}})(x\in  A) \right\}
$$
$$
 y\in \bigcup \mathfrak{F} \iff(\forall A^{\in  \mathfrak{F}})(y\in A)
$$
We often consider the family $\mathfrak{F}$ to be [[Sets of Indices|indexed]]. That is, we assume given a set $I$ and a [[Surjective Functions|surjective]] mapping $i \mapsto A_{i}$ from $I$ to $\mathfrak{F}$, so that $\mathfrak{F}=\left\{ A_{i}\mid i \in I\right\}$
The intersection of the indexed collection is designated $\bigcap_{i \in I}A_{i}$ or $\bigcap \left\{ A_{i}\mid i \in I \right\}$
If $\mathfrak{F}$ is finite, and either it or the index set is listed, then a different notation is used for its intersection. If $\mathfrak{F}=\left\{ A,B \right\}$, we designate the intersection $A\cap B$, a notation that displays the listed names, note that we generally have:
$$
A\cap B=\{ x|x\in A\text{ and }x\in B \}
$$
If $\mathfrak{F}=\left\{ A_{i}\mid i=1,\dots,n \right\}$, we generally write $A_{1}\cap A_{2}\cap\dots \cap~A_{n}$ or $\bigcap_{i=1}^{n}A_{i}$ for $\bigcap \mathfrak{F}$
We see from the definition that $A\cap B=B\cap A$, and is thus commutative
We also have [[Empty Set|$A\cap \emptyset=\emptyset$]]
## Example
$$
\{ -2,-1,0 \}\cap \{ 0,1,2 \}=\{ 0 \}
$$
