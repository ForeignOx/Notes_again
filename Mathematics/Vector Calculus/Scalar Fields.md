A scalar field on [[Vectorspace Rn|$\mathbb{R}^{n}$]] is a map $f:\mathbb{R}^{n}\to \mathbb{R}$. So it assigns a real number to every point; e.g. the temperature of a room.
## Example
Sketch the contours ([[Curves|curves]] $f(x,y)=c$ for $c\in\mathbb{R}$) of the scalar field $f(\underline{x})=y+x^{2}$ on $\mathbb{R}^{2}$, the contours are $y+x^{2}=c \iff y=c-x^{2}$, so upside-down parabolas:
