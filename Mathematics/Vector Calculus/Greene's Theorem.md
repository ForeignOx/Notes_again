## Theorem
This is a special case of [[Stokes' Theorem|Stokes' Theorem]] for when $S\subset \mathbb{R}^{2}$. If $\underline{f}:\mathbb{R}^{2}\to \mathbb{R}^{2}$ is a [[Continuity|continuously]] [[Differentiation|differentiable]] $C^{1}$ [[Vector Fields|vector field]] and $C$ is a closed [[Curves|curve]] in $\mathbb{R}^{2}$ oriented anticlockwise, then
$$
\oint_{C}\underline{f}\cdot  \underline{\hat{t}}\, d \ell=\int _{A} \left( \frac{ \partial f_{2} }{ \partial x } -\frac{ \partial f_{1} }{ \partial y }  \right) \, dxdy 
$$
Where $A$ is the area enclosed, and the integrand is the $z$-componnent of $\underline{\nabla} \times \underline{f}$
