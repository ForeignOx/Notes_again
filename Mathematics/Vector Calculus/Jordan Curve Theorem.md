## Theorem
Let $\gamma$ be a [[Curves|simple closed path]] in $\mathbb{C}$, then the complement $\mathbb{C}\setminus \gamma$ is the disjoint union of two [[domains|domains]] exactly one of which is [[Boundedness|bounded]]
## Notation
We call the bounded domain $D_{\gamma}$, or the inside of $\gamma$
We say $w\in \mathbb{C}$ is inside $\gamma$ if $w\in D_{\gamma}$
We say $w\in \mathbb{C}$ is outside $\gamma$ if $w\in\mathbb{C}\setminus(D_{\gamma}\cup\gamma)$
We say that a function $f$ is [[Holomorphicity|holomorphic]] on and inside $\gamma$ if there exists a domain $D$ which it is holomorphic, satisfying
$$
D\supset  D_{\gamma}\cup\gamma
$$
