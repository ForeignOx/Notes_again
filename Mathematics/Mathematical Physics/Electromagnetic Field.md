The electromagnetic field exerts a Lorentz force on a point particle of charge $q$:
$$
\underline{F}_{L}=q(\underline{E}(t,\underline{x}(t))+\underline{\dot{x}}(t)\times \underline{B}(t,\underline{x}(t)))
$$
One work of many people showed that a charge density $\rho(t,\underline{x})$ and current density $\underline{J}(t,\underline{x})$, then the field obeys the following equations, known as Maxwell's Equations:
Gauss' Law:
$$
\underline{\nabla} \cdot \underline{E}(t,\underline{x})  = \frac{\rho(t,\underline{x})}{\varepsilon}
$$
Gauss' Law for Magnetic Fields:
$$
\underline{\nabla} \cdot \underline{B}(t,\underline{x}) =0
$$
Faraday's Law:
$$
\underline{\nabla} \times \underline{E} (t,\underline{x})=-\frac{ \partial \underline{B}(t,\underline{x}) }{ \partial t } 
$$
And Ampere's/ Maxwell's law:
$$
\underline{\nabla} \times \underline{ B} (t,\underline{x})=\mu_{0}\left( \underline{J}(t,\underline{x})+\varepsilon_{0} \frac{ \partial \underline{E}(t,\underline{x}) }{ \partial t }  \right)
$$
In real life,
$$
\varepsilon_{0}=8.9 \times 10^{-12}\pu{Fm^{-2}}
$$
Which is known as the vacuum permittivity or the permittivity of free space
$$
\mu_{0}=4\pi \times 10^{-7} \pu{TmA^{-1}}
$$
Which is known as the vacuum permeability or the permeability of free spaceC
