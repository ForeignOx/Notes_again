## Definition
The space $L^{r}$ (which  stands for Lebesgue) with $r>0$ a number
We say that a [[Random Variables|random variable]] $X$ is in $L^{r}$ if  the [[Expectation|expectation]] of $\left| X \right|^{r}$ is finite: $\mathbb{E}(\left| X \right|^{r})<\infty$
## Example
For example if $r=1$, then $\mathbb{E}(\left| X \right|)<\infty$ would mean $X\in L$, if $r=2$, then $\mathbb{E}(\left| X \right|^{2})<\infty$ would mean $X \in L^{2}$
