## Definition
The law of a random variable $X$ is the collection of probabilities $\mathbb{P}(X\in \mathcal{U})$ for every open $\mathcal{U}\subseteq \mathbb{R}$
Two random variables $X$ and $Y$ are equal in law written
$$
X\overset{\text{law}}=Y
$$
if $\mathbb{P}(X \in\mathcal{U})=\mathbb{P}(Y\in \mathcal{U})$ for all open sets $\mathcal{U}\in \mathbb{R}$
Another word for law is distribution
Recall the [[Cumulative Distribution Functions|Cumulative distribution function]] $F_{X}(y)=\mathbb{P}(X\leq y)$ for $y\in \mathbb{R}$, this uniquely determnes the law of $X$
